The real GDP per capital is obtained by dividing the total economic output of a country adjusted for
inflation by its population. It’s an indicator of economic growth and development that leads to an
improvement in standards of living. It help to measure the economic well-being of the citizens
in a country. The growth rate of real GDP per capital implies that there is an increase in income
that leads to an increase in standard of living. People in a country can access better services
due to increase in disposable income .Additionally, unemployment is reduced when a country’s
experiences growth in its real GDP per capital thus improving people’s standard of living.
